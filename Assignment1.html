Multigrain Dosa

Multi-Grain Dosa is a nutritious and delicious South Indian dish made from a combination of different grains and lentils.
It's a healthier alternative to traditional dosa, as it incorporates a variety of grains and is rich in fiber and nutrients.
Here's a basic recipe for making Multi-Grain Dosa.

Ingredients

1 cup rice (you can use any variety)

1/2 cup whole wheat grains

1/4 cup oats

1/4 cup quinoa

1/4 cup split black gram (urad dal)

1/4 cup split chickpeas (chana dal)

1/4 cup split green gram (moong dal)

1/4 cup fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)

1/2 cup sour yogurt

Salt to taste

Water as needed

Dosa preparation

Drain the soaked grains and lentils. In a blender or wet grinder, add the drained grains and lentils along with sour yogurt and a little water.
Grind them to a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to regular dosa batter, not too thick nor too thin.
Transfer the batter to a large mixing bowl. Add salt and mix well. You can add a little water if the batter is too thick.

After fermentation, the batter will be bubbly and airy. Give it a gentle stir.
Heat a non-stick dosa tava (griddle) over medium-high heat. Grease it lightly with oil or ghee.
Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tava and quickly spread it in a circular motion to make a thin dosa.
Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa.
Cook the dosa until the edges start to lift and the bottom becomes golden and crispy.
Flip the dosa and cook the other side for a minute or so until it's lightly browned.
Remove the multi-grain dosa from the tava and serve hot.
